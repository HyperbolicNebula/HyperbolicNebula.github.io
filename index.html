The Clothing Lab

What Is The Clothing Lab?

The Clothing Lab is a project that seeks to unravel the mass-produced pieces that very often don't fit our needs. Clothes and fashion try to impose a new reality upon our bodies as opposed to conforming to their surfaces. Nevertheless, as part of the human right to health and well-being, clothing should in fact help us feel good about ourselves and our bodies. Frankly, your life is a work of art: I want to help you make more of it.

Far too many people suffer from the clothing headache that includes:
- finding clothing we like
- fitting rooms and crowds
- size, fit, and shape
- dressing difficulty
- excessive shrinkage
- garment longevity
- repair difficulty

As a matter of fact, some of the largest issues with clothing are that they don't bring us joy and that ultimately it's difficult to find something we truly like. The Clothing Lab specifically seeks to cater to those who have specific difficulties with clothing, whether that's regarding fit, size, function, dressing ease, gender, or style. Tackling such issues is a process of creating and wearing joy, even when it feels far out of reach.

Clothing is not rocket science. You don't need a Ph.D to repair a seam, but companies have complicated the lifecycle of garments. Concededely, there exists a hint of math and science to completely understand textiles, dyes, and curvature, but that's why you can come to The Clothing Lab. Ultimately, garments become the body, the art, and the life we breathe into them.

What Services Will The Clothing Lab Offer?

The main purpose of The Clothing Lab is to produce high quality, practical, and custom garments. That is to say, that I want to make a garment tailored for you. This is a long-term goal that is reflected by its inspiration and purpose, but requires learning, research, and experimenting to refine a reliable product. Willing participants, inquire within!

The Clothing Lab would also like to provide alteration and tailoring services to correct fit, shape, and size, will be a critical pathway as a more affordable and sustainable alternative to creating an entirely new garment.
